🚗 How to Install Minecraft Car Mods — Full Guide 2024

Getting Lamborghinis, Ferraris, and BMWs into your Minecraft world takes about 10 minutes. Here's the exact process, from zero to driving a Bugatti Chiron through your Minecraft city.

Step-by-Step Installation

1
Choose Your Minecraft Version
For the most car packs, use MC 1.18.2 or 1.20.1. These have the widest Immersive Vehicles support. Note: car packs only work on the same MC version as the base mod.
2
Install Minecraft Forge
Go to files.minecraftforge.net → select your MC version → download "Recommended" installer. Run the .jar installer, select "Install client" → OK. Forge adds itself to your launcher.
3
Download Immersive Vehicles
Go to curseforge.com → search "Immersive Vehicles" (or "MTS"). Download the version matching your Forge/MC version. You'll get a .jar file.
4
Download Car Pack(s)
Search CurseForge for "MTS supercar pack", "MTS luxury vehicles", or similar. Each car pack is a separate .jar file. Download the ones you want — you can have multiple packs active simultaneously.
5
Place All .jar Files in /mods
Open your .minecraft folder (Windows: %appdata%\.minecraft | Mac: ~/Library/Application Support/minecraft). Find or create the "mods" folder. Drag ALL .jar files into it — base mod + all car packs.
6
Launch with Forge Profile
Open the Minecraft Launcher. In the top-left dropdown, select the Forge profile for your version. Click Play. The game loads slightly slower with mods — this is normal.
7
Spawn Your Car In-Game
In Creative mode: open inventory → search the car name. In Survival: craft using the Workbench recipe (check the mod's JEI integration). Right-click to enter vehicle. W/A/S/D to drive.
Pro tip: Install JEI (Just Enough Items) mod alongside Immersive Vehicles to see all vehicle crafting recipes in-game. Without JEI, you have to look up recipes manually on the mod wiki.
Only download from CurseForge or Modrinth. Never download .jar mod files from unofficial sites, YouTube video descriptions, or Discord shares from untrusted users. Malicious mods can be Java executables that compromise your system.
